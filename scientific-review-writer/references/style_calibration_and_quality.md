# Style Calibration And Writing Quality

Use this when the user provides previous writing samples or wants the review to fit a specific author voice.

## Priority Order

1. Scientific accuracy and evidence boundaries.
2. Target journal or discipline conventions.
3. Manuscript type and audience.
4. User's writing style.

Personal style never overrides evidence integrity or target-journal requirements.

## Style Calibration

If the user provides at least three past writing samples, extract a soft style profile:

- sentence length and rhythm,
- preferred transitions,
- density of technical terms,
- citation integration style,
- stance toward uncertainty,
- heading style,
- figure-caption style.

Apply the profile lightly. Do not imitate distinctive phrases or copy wording.

## Writing Quality Check

During self-review, check:

- Overused generic academic phrases.
- Uniform paragraph length.
- Repeated sentence structures.
- Throat-clearing openings.
- Unnecessary intensifiers.
- Overuse of em dashes or parenthetical asides.
- Synonym cycling that changes meaning.
- Missing caveats where evidence is indirect.

The goal is clearer scientific prose, not hiding AI use.
