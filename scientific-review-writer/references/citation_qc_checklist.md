# Citation And Manuscript QC Checklist

## Literature And Claims

- Style-learning gate completed or explicitly waived by user-provided exemplars.
- Topic-learning gate completed or user-provided source corpus verified.
- Core evidence set selected.
- Access boundary recorded.
- No claim of reading unavailable full texts.
- Every literature-dependent claim has a nearby citation.
- Direct evidence and indirect evidence are distinguished.
- Unsupported claims are softened or removed.

## References

- All cited numbers have reference entries.
- All reference entries are cited.
- Numbering follows first appearance unless target style differs.
- Core references have checked authors, title, journal, year, volume, pages/article number, and DOI when needed.
- Target style is applied consistently.
- Nature-family outputs do not mix Vancouver-style `year;volume:pages` formatting with Nature-style `volume, pages (year)` formatting.
- Online-first references without stable volume/page metadata keep a DOI or full URL plus year.

## Manuscript

- Title, abstract, headings, figures, outlook, and questions support the same thesis.
- Scope does not collapse into one method, system, data type, population, organism, case, or assay unless the title explicitly narrows it.
- Mechanistic sections are organized by causal logic rather than by a list of papers.
- The formal manuscript has no workflow notes, prompt traces, claim-boundary labels, or internal QC comments.

## Figures And Tables

- Every display item is cited in text.
- Captions appear below figures.
- Figure and table vocabulary matches the manuscript.
- Labels are readable and correctly spelled.
- Figures and tables do not introduce unsupported claims.
