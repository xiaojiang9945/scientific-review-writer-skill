# Workspace-Derived Review Lessons

Use these lessons when revising or drafting plant-science review manuscripts after scanning local review projects in the current workspace.

## Scope Discipline

- Do not let a convenient assay redefine the topic. If a review concerns a broad trait, check whether the manuscript has collapsed onto one measurement axis such as aging tolerance, transcript abundance, a single stress screen, or one model organism.
- State the trait's expression contexts early. For crop traits, include field or management contexts when they are part of the biological meaning, such as abiotic-stress emergence, product quality, pathogen spectrum, seedbed condition, or regional deployment.
- When adding a new evidence axis, revise the abstract, headings, mechanism section, tables, outlook, and figures together. A late paragraph alone does not fix a narrowed thesis.

## Mechanism Depth

- Mechanism sections should explain causal architecture, not accumulate names. Organize by tissue, organ, developmental phase, environmental filter, causal gate, threshold, or signal-to-phenotype transition.
- For crop physiology and breeding reviews, connect mechanisms to the organ that expresses the trait. Examples include root architecture, coleoptile growth, vascular transport, seed coat/endosperm constraints, spike tissues, canopy temperature, grain endosperm, or reproductive organs.
- Separate three layers in the prose: measured phenotype, plausible mechanism, and breeding or management implication.
- When mechanisms remain partly unresolved, frame the uncertainty as a testable model or assay design, not as a decorative gap statement.

## Evidence Updates During Revision

- When a user identifies a conceptual weakness, run a targeted literature-refresh step before rewriting. Record query themes, public sources used, core new references, and access limits.
- Prefer recent direct crop/species evidence, but use cross-crop or model evidence when it clarifies mechanisms not yet resolved in the focal crop. Label this evidence boundary in the manuscript.
- Do not claim full-text reading of subscription-only databases or paywalled articles unless access was actually available.
- Add references where the new knowledge appears. Maintain numbered citation order and citation/reference parity after the revision.

## Figures

- Regenerate or add figures when the manuscript logic changes. Updating a caption is not enough if the image still represents the old thesis.
- Image prompts must come from the revised section and caption. Include required morphology, allowed labels, causal arrows, and elements to avoid.
- For crop figures, inspect organism morphology manually. Wheat and other cereals need narrow leaves, fibrous roots, cereal grains, spikes or spikelets as appropriate; avoid broadleaf seedling icons.
- Remove or regenerate figures with stray panel letters, thick line weights, misspelled labels, decorative legends, inconsistent palettes, or unsupported molecules/pathways.
- If an image2 output is otherwise correct but has an expendable bottom legend or margin text, a clean crop can be acceptable; local patching of biological elements is discouraged because it often leaves artifacts.
- Keep figure color, line weight, label density, and background style consistent across the set.

## Outlook And Questions

- Make the outlook more decisive by naming environment-specific ideotypes, deployment filters, predictive passports, validation networks, or decision frameworks where the evidence supports them.
- Avoid unsupported futurism. Bold outlooks should still be tied to a measurable bottleneck, a dataset, an assay, a breeding decision, or a validation design.
- Outstanding Questions should be answerable and specific enough to imply an experiment, dataset, or selection pipeline.

## Final QC

Run at least three revision rounds for major manuscript updates:

1. Citation and format: abstract length, citation/reference parity, first-appearance order, figure/table count, all image paths resolved.
2. Logic and language: thesis alignment across title, abstract, headings, mechanisms, tables, figures, and outlook; no placeholders, reviewer-response phrasing, generic claims, or intermediate generated text.
3. Figure and DOCX integrity: inspect final images for morphology, line weight, stray letters, spelling, and label fit; rebuild Word; verify embedded image count, table count, captions, references heading, and image aspect ratios.

Record the QC results in a short project note or log when the deliverable is a finished manuscript.
