# Structure And Style

## Default Manuscript Shape

1. Title.
2. Abstract.
3. Keywords.
4. Introduction or background with term boundaries and thesis.
5. Measurement, phenotype, environment, or conceptual architecture.
6. Deep mechanism organized by causal layers.
7. Species, crop, tissue, or system diversity.
8. Breeding, engineering, biotechnology, management, or translational pipeline when relevant.
9. Outlook or conclusion with named bottlenecks.
10. Outstanding Questions.
11. References.

## Length Targets

Use target-journal instructions first. If no target is given:

- Abstract: 180-250 words.
- Keywords: 5-7 terms.
- Introduction/background: 800-1200 words for broad reviews.
- Main mechanism section: usually the longest scientific section.
- Outlook: 450-700 words for full reviews.
- Outstanding Questions: 6-8.
- References: at least 30 for a complete review; 35-60 is typical for a figure-rich manuscript.

## Style

- Use concise, claim-led section openings.
- Prefer synthesis over paper-by-paper listing.
- Define terms when confusion would change interpretation.
- Make transitions explain why the next section follows.
- Avoid workflow phrases such as "the following section will," "this generated draft," "claim boundary," and "as requested."
- Avoid reviewer-response phrasing inside the manuscript.

## Abstract

A strong abstract should cover:

1. Field problem or biological tension.
2. Recent conceptual or technical shift.
3. Synthesis scope.
4. Key mechanism, framework, or tool axis.
5. Concrete bottleneck or decision problem.

## Outlook

The outlook should name actionable bottlenecks, testable hypotheses, decision frameworks, and validation designs. Do not end with a generic "more research is needed" unless followed by a specific method or uncertainty.
