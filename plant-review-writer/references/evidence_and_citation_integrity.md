# Evidence And Citation Integrity

## Literature Gates

For a full review, document:

- Style-learning records: at least 200 relevant review records or approved exemplars.
- Topic-learning records: at least 200 topic-relevant literature records before drafting.
- Query families and sources searched.
- Inclusion logic.
- Access boundary.
- Core evidence set selected for citation.

## Evidence Hierarchy

Use the strongest evidence available for the claim:

1. Direct evidence in the focal species, crop, tissue, process, or system.
2. Closely related crops or plant systems.
3. General plant model evidence.
4. Non-plant or general eukaryotic evidence only where it clarifies conserved logic.

Do not write model-system evidence as if it has been directly shown in the focal crop.

## Citation Placement

- Put citations at the exact knowledge point they support.
- Avoid paragraph-end citation pools.
- Keep citation numbering and reference order consistent.
- Check every numbered citation has a reference entry and every reference entry is cited.
- Verify DOI, title, journal, year, and author metadata for the core references.

## Claim Calibration

Use language that matches the evidence:

- Direct evidence: shows, demonstrates, resolves, quantifies, maps.
- Strong but incomplete evidence: supports, suggests, is consistent with, nominates.
- Synthesis: couples, constrains, buffers, coordinates, tunes, partitions, rewires.
- Unknown mechanism: may, could, remains unresolved, requires direct testing.
