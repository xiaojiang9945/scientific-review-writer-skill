# Five-Round QC

Run these checks before delivering a substantial manuscript or public demo.

## Round 1: Literature And Citations

- Style-learning gate completed and documented.
- Topic-learning gate completed and documented.
- Core evidence set selected.
- Access limits stated.
- Citations placed near supported claims.
- Citation and reference lists match.
- Numbered citations follow first appearance when required.

## Round 2: Structure And Thesis

- Title, abstract, keywords, headings, figures, outlook, and questions point to the same thesis.
- The manuscript scope does not collapse into one assay, organism, stress, tissue, or marker unless the title intentionally narrows it.
- Each section has a clear role.
- Mechanism sections are organized by causal logic rather than a list of genes or papers.

## Round 3: Language

- No workflow narration in the manuscript.
- No claim-boundary labels.
- No prompt traces or intermediate-generation notes.
- No reviewer-response phrasing.
- Evidence wording matches evidence strength.
- Species names and gene or locus formatting are checked.

## Round 4: Figures And Tables

- Every figure and table is cited in the text.
- Captions appear below figures.
- Figure terminology matches the manuscript.
- Labels are readable and spelled correctly.
- Line weights, background, and palette are consistent.
- Figures do not introduce unsupported claims.

## Round 5: Final Package, Privacy, And Export

- Markdown or DOCX renders with all figures.
- DOCX, when produced, has embedded figures and preserved aspect ratios.
- No local absolute paths.
- No account names, tokens, API keys, private notes, or raw full-text archives.
- Release status and version are accurate.
